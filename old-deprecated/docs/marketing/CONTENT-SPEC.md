## 2. Voice & Tone

### 2.1 Core Tone Attributes

| Attribute        | What it means in practice                                                                                                   |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Confident**    | State benefits as facts, not hedges. "Grade in 8 minutes" not "can help reduce grading time."                               |
| **Direct**       | Lead with the benefit, not the feature. "Teachers get their weekends back" not "AI automates the grading pipeline."         |
| **Empathetic**   | We understand teachers are overworked, students are anxious, parents are worried. Acknowledge the pain before the solution. |
| **Grounded**     | Every claim is backed by a specific feature or number from the codebase. No marketing fluff.                                |
| **Approachable** | Professional but not stiff. This is education, not enterprise software. We care about students.                             |
| **Inspiring**    | We're not selling software. We're selling better outcomes for kids. End on aspiration, not features.                        |

### 2.2 Writing Principles

1. **Benefit first, feature second.** "Students get 24/7 help from an AI tutor"
   before "AI Tutor with Socratic dialogue."
2. **Specifics beat generalities.** "8 minutes" beats "faster." "15 question
   types" beats "many question types."
3. **Short sentences win.** If a sentence needs more than two commas, split it.
4. **Active voice always.** "AI grades your exam" not "your exam is graded by
   AI."
5. **Use 'you' and 'your'.** Speak directly to the reader. "Your teachers" not
   "teachers."
6. **Avoid jargon.** No "LLM", "OCR", "CDN", "multi-tenant SaaS" in
   public-facing copy. Translate to outcomes.
7. **Numbers in numerals.** "15 question types" not "fifteen question types."
   Numbers are proof points, make them scan-able.
8. **One idea per paragraph.** Each paragraph should make exactly one point.

### 2.3 Language to Use vs. Avoid

| Instead of...                | Use...                                                         |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "AI-powered" (overused)      | "AI that actually works", "intelligent", specific feature name |
| "Innovative", "cutting-edge" | Specific capability — let the features speak                   |
| "Solution"                   | The actual product name (LvlUp Spaces, AutoGrade)              |
| "Leverage"                   | "Use"                                                          |
| "Utilize"                    | "Use"                                                          |
| "Seamless"                   | Describe the actual experience                                 |
| "Robust"                     | Describe the specific capability                               |
| "World-class"                | Specific benchmark or proof point                              |
| "LLM" / "OCR" / "NLP"        | "AI", "Gemini Vision", "reading the paper"                     |
| "Multi-tenant SaaS"          | "Each school's data is fully isolated"                         |

## 3. Target Audiences

### 3.1 Primary Decision-Maker — School Principal / Academic Director

**Profile:** Makes the purchase decision. Cares about school performance,
teacher retention, parent satisfaction, cost accountability, and institutional
reputation.

**Core fears:** Wasted budget on tools that don't work. Teachers who leave due
to burnout. Parents who lose trust. Students who fall through the cracks and
hurt metrics.

**Core desires:** Measurable improvement in results. Happy teachers who stay.
Informed parents who trust the school. Cost-visible AI adoption.

**What resonates:**

- School-wide performance metrics and at-risk visibility
- AI cost transparency (no surprise invoices)
- Easy onboarding and full data export for board reviews
- The fact that teachers maintain academic authority (AI assists, humans decide)

**Primary message for this audience:**

> Auto-LevelUp gives your school the infrastructure to run on data, not instinct
> — with full cost visibility, granular staff controls, and the analytics to
> identify what's working and what isn't before it becomes a problem.

### 3.2 Teacher

**Profile:** Subject matter expert, time-poor, increasingly expected to
personalize learning and provide fast feedback across 100+ students
simultaneously.

**Core fears:** More work, not less. Tools that replace their judgment. Unfair
AI grading they can't control. Marking weekends.

**Core desires:** Time back. Insight without manual effort. Tools that make them

**Primary message for this audience:**

> Auto-LevelUp gives you back the time you spend on marking so you can spend it
> on teaching. The AI grades, you review the edge cases. You always have the
> final word.

**Profile:** Under pressure, often doesn't know where to start when stuck,

**Core fears:** Falling behind without knowing it. Not understanding something

**Core desires:** Knowing where they stand. Getting help when they're stuck.

## 4. The Problem Framework

### 4.1 The Four Core Problems

Use these consistently. Do not invent new problem framings.

**Problem 1: The Grading Bottleneck**

> A teacher with 5 classes spends entire weekends marking handwritten answer
> papers — time stolen from lesson prep, student support, and personal life.
> Feedback arrives days after the exam, when the learning moment has passed.

**Problem 2: One-Pace-Fits-All Learning**

> Every student in the same class moves through the same material at the same
> speed. Students who grasp concepts quickly disengage. Students who struggle
> fall behind silently. Neither reaches their potential.

**Problem 3: Invisible Struggling Students**

> The student who is quietly falling behind doesn't raise their hand. Declining
> quiz scores, missed practice sessions, and growing knowledge gaps go unnoticed
> until intervention requires crisis management — not early support.

**Problem 4: Parents Informed Too Late**

> Families receive meaningful information about their child's academic health
> once per term — at report card time. By then, a correctable trend has become
> an entrenched problem. Parents are reacting to failure instead of preventing
> it.

### 4.2 Cost of Inaction (for decision-makers)

> Schools that don't solve these problems see: higher teacher burnout and
> turnover, lower student outcomes, declining parent trust, and performance gaps
> that compound year over year.

### 6.2 AutoGrade

**Canonical one-paragraph description:**

> AutoGrade is an AI exam grading platform for schools. Teachers scan
> handwritten answer sheets with any smartphone, and the platform automatically
> extracts questions from the question paper, maps each student's answers to the
> correct question, and grades every response using a configurable
> multi-dimensional rubric — producing per-student, per-question feedback
> reports in 8–15 minutes for an entire class.

#### Feature Descriptions (canonical one-liners)

| Feature                       | Canonical Description                                                                                                                                                |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Scanner App**               | A mobile PWA — any phone, no installation — that guides staff through a 4-step process to scan and upload answer sheets                                              |
| **Web Upload**                | A drag-and-drop upload path in the admin dashboard for desktop-based submission handling                                                                             |
| **OCR Question Extraction**   | Gemini Vision reads the question paper image and extracts all questions, marks, and rubric criteria in under 60 seconds                                              |
| **Panopticon Answer Mapping** | AI reads the full question paper and answer sheet simultaneously and maps each student answer to its question — regardless of page order                             |
| **RELMS Grading Engine**      | A multi-dimensional AI grading engine that evaluates each question with a configurable rubric across 4 dimensions: Critical Issues, Completeness, Structure, Clarity |
| **4 Rubric Modes**            | Criteria-based, dimension-based, holistic, and hybrid — a different mode for every subject and exam type                                                             |
| **Per-Question Feedback**     | Every graded question returns: score, rubric breakdown, strengths, improvement steps, key takeaway, mistake classification, and confidence score                     |
| **Manual Override**           | Teachers can override any AI grade with one click; reason is required and stored — full audit trail maintained                                                       |
| **Bulk Parallel Grading**     | 40 students × 10 questions = 400 grading tasks processed simultaneously. Real-time progress counter.                                                                 |
| **Dead Letter Queue**         | Failed grading tasks are isolated, flagged, and manually retryable — one bad scan never stops the entire exam                                                        |
| **PDF Grade Reports**         | Auto-generated printable reports with full feedback, LaTeX math rendering, and adjustable display settings                                                           |
| **AI Cost Tracking**          | Every Gemini API call logged with token counts and USD cost — per-call, per-exam, per-submission, per-month                                                          |
| **Evaluation Settings**       | Named rubric profiles per exam type — configure dimensions, strictness, and feedback fields once; apply per exam                                                     |
| **Multi-Tenant Security**     | Each school's data is fully isolated. Schools bring their own Gemini API key; costs go to their own billing account.                                                 |
| **Student Result Portal**     | Students access their own graded papers and feedback 24/7 via the student-facing portal                                                                              |

#### The AutoGrade Pipeline (5 steps — always describe in this order)

1. **Scan** — Teacher uses any phone to photograph answer sheets via the guided
   PWA app
2. **Extract** — Gemini Vision reads the question paper, extracts all questions
   and rubric criteria (< 60 seconds)
3. **Map** — Panopticon AI maps each student answer to the correct question,
   regardless of page order
4. **Grade** — RELMS engine grades every question in parallel with
   multi-dimensional rubric scoring
5. **Review & Release** — Teacher reviews, overrides where needed, releases
   results to students with PDF reports

#### The 4 RELMS Feedback Dimensions

| Dimension                   | Priority | What it checks                                                            |
| --------------------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Critical Issues**         | HIGH     | Technical accuracy, missing concepts, formula errors, logical flaws       |
| **Completeness**            | HIGH     | All sub-parts answered, verification steps included, final answer present |
| **Structure & Flow**        | MEDIUM   | Organization, step sequencing, proper format                              |
| **Clarity & Communication** | MEDIUM   | Notation consistency, units, variable naming, readability                 |

- **Primary audience:** Prospective schools doing due diligence before a

**Cold email length:** Max 150 words. One CTA only.

- Length: 150–200 words

**Twitter/X:**

## 13. What to Avoid

### Factual claims to never make without verification

- Specific price/cost numbers (not confirmed)
- Number of schools currently using the platform
- Government certification or accreditation claims
- "Industry-leading" or comparative claims without evidence

### Tone pitfalls

- **Don't make AI sound infallible.** Always acknowledge teacher override. "AI
  grades, teachers decide" is the correct framing.
- **Don't make it sound like teachers are being replaced.** The message is
  always: AI does the repetitive work, teachers do the judgment work.
- **Don't use fear alone.** Acknowledge the problem, but always move quickly to
  the solution.
- **Don't over-promise on student outcomes.** We improve the conditions for
  learning. We don't guarantee results.

### Design/visual guardrails

- Never use the full product name "Auto-LevelUp" in all caps
- Never write it as "Auto Level Up", "AutoLevelUp", or "auto-levelup"
- Always show both pillars together when introducing the platform — never
  present them as separate products
- Maintain the blue/indigo (LvlUp) + teal/green (AutoGrade) color association
  established in the pamphlet and brochure
