Explain why the moon shows phases, and why we always…

The moon shows phases because half of it is always lit by the sun, but as it orbits Earth we see different fractions of that lit half. When the moon sits between Earth and the sun we see the dark side — a new moon. When Earth is in the middle, the whole lit face points at us — full moon.

We always see the same side because the moon is tidally locked: its rotation period matches its orbital period
